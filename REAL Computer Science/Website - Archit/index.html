As the regular season comes to an end, I am going to be preparing you for the playoffs by ranking the top 5 players in the national hockey leauge.

#5: Erik Karlsson, D, Ottawa Senators

Honours and Achievements

  1. 3x NHL All Star
  2. 2x James Norris Memorial Trophy Winner
  3. 3x Viking Award Winner

The topper of our list is nonother than, Erik Karlsson. Erik Karlsson was drafted 15 overall, back in 2008, by the Ottawa Senators. He is well known around the league as one of the most dominant offensive defensemen with his amazing passing vision and his accurate shot. The 27-year-old had at least 66 points, 16 goals, 45 assists and 26 power-play points each of the past four seasons

#4: Steven Stamkos, C, Tampa Bay Lightning

Honours and Achievements

  1. 5x NHL All Star
  2. 2x Maurice "Rocket" Richard Trophy Winner

After Erik Karlsson comes a veteran center by the name of Steven Stamkos. Steven Stamkos was the first overall pick in the 2006 Ontario Hockey League (OHL) draft. Playing with the Sarnia Sting of the OHL draft, he scored 100 goals over two years. After a successful OHL career, Stamkos was selected first overall in the 2008 NHL Entry Draft by the Tampa Bay Lightning. No known so much about his passing, his shot is his deadliest weapon, especially on the powerplay. With this shot, he his known as an elite scorer around the league.

#3: Nikita Kucherov, RW, Tampa Bay Lightning

Honours and Achievements

  1. 1x NHL Plus-Minus Award
  2. 2x NHL All Star
  3. 1x U-18 All Star Team

The player that takes the 3rd spot on this list is a very underrated player in the league that is Steven Stamkos' right wing, Nikita Kucherov. Although he doesn't have many awards or achievements, he is still one of the best players in the league. This season, in 58 games played, he has an outstanding league leading 76 points! This means he is averaging about 1.3 points per game! With numbers like this, there is no doubt he is one of the leagues best players.

#2: Sidney Crosby, C, Pittsburgh Penguins

Honours and Achievements

  1. 3x Stanley Cup Champion
  2. 2x Conn Smythe Trophy Winner
  3. 4x Prince of Wales Trophy Winner
  4. 2x Art Ross Trophy Winner
  5. 2x Maurice "Rocket" Richard Trophy Winner
  6. 3x Ted Lindsay Award Winner
  7. 2x Hart Memorial Trophy Winner
  8. 7x NHL All Star

Sidney Crosby is arguably one of the best players in the league and one of the best to play the game. From June 2016 through June 2017, Crosby racked up the hardware. He was Penguins captain for back-to-back Stanley Cup championships and won the Conn Smythe Trophy as playoff MVP following each championship. He also had 10 points (three goals, seven assists) in six games as captain of World Cup of Hockey 2016 champion Team Canada and won tournament MVP. Even though his career has been filled with many injuries, he is still consistently racking up superior numbers.

#1: Connor McDavid, C, Edmonton Oilers

Honours and Achievements

  1. 1x NHL All Star
  2. 1x Art Ross Trophy
  3. 1x Hart Memorial Trophy
  4. 1x Ted Lindsay Award

Finally, the person taking the top spot on this list is none other than, Connor McDavid. Mcdavid was drafted 1st overall by the Edmonton Oilers back in 2015. Prior to getting drafted, he was one of the most anticipated players to play the game and he was doing scout since he was only 13 years old! Although his rookie season in the NHL was stopped abruptly by a shoulder injury, he managed in his second year to get the Art Ross Trophy, Ted Lindsy Award, and the Heart Memorial trophy. He also managed to be a 2x All-Star and is known as the league's fastest skater.
